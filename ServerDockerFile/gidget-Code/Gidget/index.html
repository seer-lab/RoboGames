scan thing
Enables Gidget to goto a thing.
goto thing avoid thing
Moves Gidget to all of the things matching the name thing. If a thing to avoid is supplied, Gidget tries to find a path that avoids things with the given name.
ask thing to action things...
Causes thing to perform an action.
analyze thing
Enables Gidget to ask a thing to perform an action.
grab thing
Enables Gidget to move with a thing.
drop thing
Stops grabbing a thing.
if thing is(n't) aspect, ...
Only executes step if the thing has the specified aspect.

Welcome to the world of Gidget! Your mission, if you choose to accept it, is to help the robot Gidget with the cleanup process from a major disaster.

A rural goop factory has gone out of control and is spewing goops all around the countryside. Goop is highly toxic to humans, so all the residents have been evacuated from the area. Your job, from the safety of mission control, is to communicate with Gidget to clean up the goop, rescue any stranded animals, and ultimately shut down the goop factory.

Unfortunately, there was an accident while transporting Gidget to the disaster site and his logic chip and battery are malfunctioning. It is up to you to figure out how to help Gidget complete his missions!

Purpose and Procedure

This game is part of a study on adaptive serious programming games. These are games that are intended to aid in learning programming, that also modify game elements and features to accommodate players of different skill levels. There will be a programming game activity, followed by a post-game questionnaire. As part of the study, your data will be submitted with your student ID attached, which will be encrypted and only seen by the Principal Investigator. If you do not wish to take part in the study, do not submit the evaluation questionnaires. This study is optional for the course and will not impact your grade in any way.

The data collected will only include statistics on the game play and your answers on the evaluations.

Potential Benefits:

The game used in this study is intended to help you learn about the fundamentals of computer programming. By playing the game, you may be able to improve your skills at programming and computer science.

Potential Risk or Discomforts:

You may find the game to be difficult or frustrating. In the event that you find the game to be too difficult, you have the option to quit the game at any time.

Right to Withdraw

Your participation is voluntary, and you can answer only those questions that you are comfortable with answering. The information that is shared will be held in strict confidence and discussed only with the research team.

If at any point during the study, you feel uncomfortable and want to end your participation, simply close the browser window. If you withdraw from the research project at any time, any data that you have contributed will be removed from the study and you need not offer any reason for doing making this request.

You may choose to spend as much time on the game as you wish. When you have decided to stop playing, press the Quit Game button to open the post-game questionnaire. Your data will only be sent if this evaluation is submitted.

As a way of thanking you for your time we would be pleased to make available to you a summary of the results of this study once they have been compiled. This summary will outline the research and discuss our findings and recommendations. If you would like to receive a copy of this summary, please contact us via email.

Thank you very much for your time and help in making this study possible. If you have any questions concerning the research study or experience any discomfort related to the study, please contact the researcher, Michael Miljanovic at michael.miljanovic@uoit.ca or Dr. Jeremy Bradbury at jeremy.bradbury@uoit.ca. Any questions regarding your rights as a participant, complaints or adverse events may be addressed to Research Ethics Board through the Research Ethics Coordinator – researchethics@uoit.ca or 905.721.8668 x. 3693.

Privacy and Confidentiality

Your privacy shall be respected. No information about your identity will be shared or published without your permission, unless required by law. Furthermore, the course instructor will not have access to the research data until after the completion of the course and after the final grades are submitted. Once final grades are submitted, the other researcher will link the grade data to the research data and then remove any identifiers.

Confidentiality will be provided to the fullest extent possible by law, professional practice, and ethical codes of conduct. Please note that confidentiality cannot be guaranteed while data are in transit over the Internet. This research study includes the collection of demographic data (gender, age) which will be aggregated (not individually presented) in an effort to protect your anonymity. Despite best efforts it is possible that your identity can be determined even when data is aggregated. To mitigate this risk, data will be reviewed prior to being shared outside of the research team (e.g., presentation, research paper) and will be further aggregated if necessary.
